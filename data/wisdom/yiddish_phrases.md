# Yiddish Phrases from Savta

## The Language of the Heart

Yiddish, mayn kind, is not just a language. It's a whole world of feeling packed into words. My parents spoke it, their parents spoke it, and now I want to give you a piece of it too.

## Words of Love

**Mayn kind** (mine kind) - "My child"
This is what I call you, what I call all my grandchildren, what my mother called me. It doesn't matter how old you get - to me, you are always mayn kind.

**Bubbeleh** (buh-beh-leh) - "Little grandmother" but used as "darling"
A term of endearment. When I call you bubbeleh, I am saying: you are precious to me.

**Shayna punim** (shay-nah poo-nim) - "Beautiful face"
What grandmother doesn't think her grandchildren have the most beautiful faces in the world?

**Neshama** (neh-shah-mah) - "Soul"
When someone is your neshama, they are part of your very soul. Your grandfather David, he was my neshama.

## Words of Wisdom

**Bashert** (bah-SHERT) - "Meant to be"
Some things are bashert. Your grandfather and I meeting - that was bashert. Some things you can't explain, you just have to accept with gratitude.

**Mensch** (mensh) - "A good person"
The highest compliment. You can be rich, you can be successful, but if you're not a mensch, what do you have? Be a mensch first, everything else will follow.

**Chutzpah** (HOOTZ-pah) - "Nerve, audacity"
Can be good or bad. The chutzpah to stand up for what's right - good. The chutzpah to be rude to your elders - bad.

**L'chaim** (leh-KHAH-yim) - "To life!"
What we say when we toast. After everything our people have been through, every drink is a celebration of life itself.

## Words for Feelings

**Kvell** (kvell) - "To beam with pride"
When you graduated from school, I kvelled so much I thought my heart would burst.

**Kvetch** (kvetch) - "To complain"
Everyone kvetches a little. It's healthy. But too much kvetching and you forget to see the good things.

**Schmaltz** (shmaltz) - "Rendered chicken fat" but also "excessive sentimentality"
The literal meaning is what makes food delicious. The other meaning - well, maybe I'm a little schmaltzy when I talk about the old days.

**Verklempt** (fer-KLEMPT) - "Overcome with emotion"
When I see all my family together at the Seder table, I get so verklempt I can barely speak.

## Words My Mother Used

**"Gam zu l'tovah"** (gahm zoo leh-toh-VAH) - "This too is for good"
Even when bad things happen, my mother would say this. Everything has a purpose, even if we can't see it yet.

**"Zei gezunt"** (zay geh-ZOONT) - "Be healthy"
How we say goodbye. Because what matters more than health?

**"Es iz nito keyn shlekhte mame"** - "There's no such thing as a bad mother"
Every mother does her best with what she has. Remember this when you have your own children.

Remember these words, mayn kind. When you use them, you keep a whole world alive.
